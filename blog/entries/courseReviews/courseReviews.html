Rate and review the courses you play

Jul 14, 2026

You have opinions about the courses you play. The greens that never held, the par 3 you still think about, the muni that punches way above its green fee. Squabbit now gives all of that a home: you can rate and review any course, and see what other golfers thought before you tee it up.

A course page worth visiting

We rebuilt the course page from the ground up. It opens with a photo header so you know at a glance where you are, and the whole page is now organized into tabs so the scorecard, the details, and the reviews each get room to breathe. No more scrolling past everything to find the one thing you came for.

Right up top, above the scorecard, is the course's rating summary: the overall score and how many golfers have weighed in. It is the first thing you see, because it is usually the first thing you want to know.

Leave your rating

Rating a course takes a few seconds. Give it a star rating, and if you want to say more, add a review with whatever you would tell a friend who asked whether it was worth the trip. Conditions, pace, value, the walk, the views: it is your call what to mention.

Read the room before you book

Every review lives in a dedicated tab on the course page. Scroll through what other golfers have said, see the overall rating they add up to, and get a real sense of a course before you ever set foot on the first tee. It is the kind of local knowledge that used to only exist in the clubhouse.

Start rating

Open any course in Squabbit, take in the new look, and leave a rating for a course you know well. The more golfers who chime in, the better every course page gets for the next person deciding where to play.
